Coordinate Systems

AutoScript uses numerical values to determine the position of objects in the microscope chamber or of geometric elements in an image. These coordinates are specified in coordinate systems that vary between AutoScript functional areas. This chapter lists the main coordinate systems in a typical SEM.

Stage coordinate system

The picture below shows the orientation of stage axes X, Y, Z, R and T. The orientation of the Z axis is reversed when the "Link Z to FWD (free working distance)" function is activated. See specimen.stage.link() and specimen.stage.unlink() for details.

Scanning reduced area coordinate system

"Reduced area" is one of the supported scanning modes. In this mode, the beam scans in a rectangular sub-region of the entire field of view. The area is specified by the coordinate of its upper left corner, width and height. The upper left corner of the current field of view has the coordinate [0, 0], the lower right corner has the coordinate [1, 1].

Patterning coordinate system

Pattern shapes for patterning jobs are created in a special coordinate system that has its origin in the center of the field of view. The position and size of patterns are specified directly in distance units (meters) bounded by the current scan field width and height.

Imaging views numbering

AutoScript shares the concept of imaging views with the main microscope user interface application "Microscope Control". There are a total of 4 views with index 1 to 4, which divide the entire screen area into 4 quadrants of equal size.
